About Us

Ratan Tata, born on December 28, 1937, in Mumbai, India, is a prominent Indian industrialist, philanthropist, and former chairman of the Tata Group, one of Indiaâ€™s largest conglomerates. He is known for his visionary leadership, which transformed the Tata Group into a global powerhouse with notable acquisitions like Jaguar Land Rover, Corus Steel, and Tetley Tea. Under his leadership, the group expanded into over 100 countries and achieved significant milestones, including the launch of the Tata Nano, the world's most affordable car. A graduate in architecture from Cornell University and an alumnus of Harvard Business School, Ratan Tata is admired for his focus on innovation, ethical business practices, and corporate social responsibility. Even after retiring as chairman in 2012, he remains active as Chairman Emeritus of Tata Sons and continues to mentor startups and invest in promising ventures through Ratan Tata Trust. Known for his humility and philanthropy, he has significantly contributed to healthcare, education, and rural development in India.

Career

Ratan Tata, born on December 28, 1937, in Mumbai, India, is a renowned industrialist who began his career at Tata Steel in 1962 and later served as the chairman of Tata Sons from 1991 to 2012, transforming the Tata Group into a global conglomerate with landmark acquisitions like Jaguar Land Rover and Corus Steel, while emphasizing innovation, ethical business practices, and philanthropy.

Personal Life

Despite his towering professional success, Dr. Ratan Tata is known for his simplicity and humility. A passionate lover of cars and architecture, he has always maintained a low profile in his personal life. His commitment to philanthropy and social causes reflects his belief in giving back to society.
